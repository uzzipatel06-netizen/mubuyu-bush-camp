Our Heritage

Born from a Love of the Wild

Mubuyu Bush Camp was founded on a simple, yet profound philosophy: that the most meaningful safari experiences are those that prioritize intimacy over scale. Nestled in the heart of Kafue National Park, our family-owned retreat is more than just a lodge; it is a sanctuary for the soul. We believe that true luxury is found in the stillness of the dawn, the expert track of a local guide, and the unhurried rhythm of the riverside.

Every detail of Mubuyu has been crafted to celebrate the raw elegance of the Zambian bush. From our four exclusive riverfront chalets to our commitment to low-impact conservation, we invite you to disconnect from the world and rediscover the authentic essence of Africa. This is not just a destination; it is an immersion into a timeless wilderness, where the spirit of the Kafue flows through everything we do.

The Destination

A Jewel in Kafue National Park

Why Kafue is Different

While Africa offers many safari destinations, Kafue National Park remains a rare frontier. It is one of the largest and most pristine wilderness areas on the continent, yet it remains blissfully uncrowded. Here, you won't find queues of vehicles at every sighting; instead, you'll find vast, untouched landscapes where the wildlife roams as it has for millennia.

Remote. Wild. Uncrowded.

Choosing Kafue means choosing authenticity. It is a destination for those who seek the thrill of discovery and the peace of solitude. At Mubuyu Bush Camp, we provide the perfect vantage point to witness this majestic ecosystem in its most pure and unadulterated form.

Seasonality Guide

Best Time to Visit Kafue National Park

Understanding the seasons in Zambia is key to planning your perfect luxury safari in Kafue National Park. Mubuyu Bush Camp operates during the prime wildlife viewing months, ensuring you get the most out of your stay.

The Green Season (April - May)

The bush is lush and vibrant following the rains. It's a photographer's paradise with clear skies, dramatic sunsets, and newborn wildlife. The river is full, making for excellent boating safaris.

Peak Winter (June - August)

Cooler temperatures and drying bush make for comfortable game drives and walking safaris. Wildlife begins to congregate around the river, offering consistent sightings of elephants and hippos.

Heat of the Season (Sept - Nov)

As the plains dry out, predator action heats up. This is the best time for dramatic wildlife encounters as animals flock to the remaining water sources. Ideal for fishing enthusiasts.
